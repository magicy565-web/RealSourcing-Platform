## What is Dropshipping?

Dropshipping is a retail fulfillment model where the seller does not hold inventory. When a customer places an order, the seller purchases the item from a third-party supplier (typically a manufacturer or wholesaler), who then ships directly to the customer. The seller never sees or handles the physical product.

**Key advantages:** No upfront inventory investment, low startup cost, ability to test many products quickly, no warehouse needed.

**Key disadvantages:** Lower margins than wholesale, less control over shipping speed and packaging, harder to differentiate from competitors selling the same products, dependency on supplier reliability.

## The China Sourcing Advantage

Sourcing directly from Chinese manufacturers (rather than using middlemen like AliExpress or Alibaba resellers) typically reduces product cost by 30–60%. This is the core value proposition of platforms like RealSourcing.

**Direct factory vs. trading company:**
- A **factory** manufactures the product themselves. Lower prices, but higher MOQ and less flexibility.
- A **trading company** buys from multiple factories and resells. Higher prices, but lower MOQ and more product variety.
- RealSourcing focuses on connecting buyers directly with factories.

## Business Models on RealSourcing

### 1. Standard Dropshipping
Order products one at a time through a fulfillment agent. No inventory. Best for testing products and early-stage sellers.

### 2. Bulk + Fulfillment Agent
Buy 50–200 units, store at a China-based fulfillment warehouse, ship individual orders as they come in. Better margins and faster shipping than pure dropshipping.

### 3. Private Label
Work with a factory to put your brand on their product (custom packaging, logo, sometimes custom design). Requires higher MOQ (typically 200–500 units) but builds brand equity and protects from price competition.

### 4. OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing)
Design your own product and have a factory manufacture it to your specifications. Highest investment, highest differentiation. Suitable for established sellers.

## Profit Margin Framework

**Gross Margin = (Selling Price − COGS) / Selling Price**

Where COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) includes:
- Factory/product cost
- Shipping from factory to fulfillment center (or direct to customer)
- Import duties and customs fees
- Fulfillment/warehousing fees (if applicable)
- Packaging and inserts

**Target margins by business model:**
- Dropshipping: 30–50% gross margin minimum (after all COGS)
- Bulk + fulfillment: 45–65% gross margin
- Private label: 60–80% gross margin

**Additional costs to factor in:**
- Platform fees: Shopify ($39–$399/month), Amazon (8–15% referral fee), Etsy (6.5% transaction fee + listing fees)
- Payment processing: ~2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (Stripe, PayPal)
- Advertising: Typically 15–30% of revenue for paid ads (Facebook, TikTok, Google)
- Returns: Budget 5–15% of revenue depending on category

## Supplier Vetting Framework

Before placing any order, evaluate a supplier on these dimensions:

| Dimension | What to Check | Red Flags |
|---|---|---|
| **Legitimacy** | Business license, factory photos, video call | No video call, refuses to show factory |
| **Responsiveness** | Reply time, quality of answers | >48h response, vague answers |
| **Sample quality** | Order a sample before bulk | Refuses samples, charges excessive sample fees |
| **FTGI Score** | Check RealSourcing FTGI rating | Score below 60 |
| **Certifications** | Relevant certs for your market | Can't provide cert documents |
| **References** | Ask for buyer references | None available |
| **Payment terms** | 30% deposit, 70% before shipment is standard | 100% upfront is a red flag |

## Chinese New Year & Factory Calendar

Chinese factories close for major holidays. Plan your inventory and orders accordingly:

| Holiday | Typical Dates | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Chinese New Year (Spring Festival) | Late Jan – mid Feb | 2–4 week factory closure, the most impactful |
| Qingming Festival | Early April | 3–5 day closure |
| Labour Day (Golden Week) | May 1–5 | 5–7 day closure |
| Dragon Boat Festival | June (varies) | 3 day closure |
| National Day (Golden Week) | Oct 1–7 | 7–10 day closure |

**Rule of thumb:** Place orders at least 6 weeks before Chinese New Year. Factories get extremely busy in November–December as buyers rush to beat the holiday closure.

## Payment & Trade Terms

### Standard Payment Terms
- **T/T (Telegraphic Transfer / Wire Transfer):** Most common. Typically 30% deposit upfront, 70% balance before shipment.
- **L/C (Letter of Credit):** Used for large orders ($50,000+). Provides payment protection for both parties.
- **PayPal / Alibaba Trade Assurance:** Offers buyer protection but adds 3–5% fees. Factories may charge extra.
- **Western Union / MoneyGram:** Avoid — no buyer protection.

### Incoterms (Shipping Terms)
- **EXW (Ex Works):** You arrange all shipping from the factory gate. Cheapest factory price, but you bear all logistics risk.
- **FOB (Free On Board):** Factory delivers to the port and loads onto the ship. You arrange ocean freight and import. Most common for experienced importers.
- **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight):** Factory arranges shipping to destination port. You handle import clearance.
- **DDP (Delivered Duty Paid):** Factory handles everything including import duties. Most convenient but most expensive. Good for beginners.

**Recommendation for beginners:** Start with DDP or CIF to simplify logistics. Move to FOB as you scale to reduce costs.

## Product Research Principles

### Winning Product Criteria
A product is worth testing if it meets most of these criteria:
- **Solves a problem or fulfills a desire** (not just "looks nice")
- **Wow factor** — customers think "I need this" when they see it
- **Not widely available in local retail stores** (reduces "I could just buy this at Target" objection)
- **Healthy margin** — can be sold at 3–5x the landed cost
- **Not too heavy or fragile** — keeps shipping costs and damage rates manageable
- **Not a brand-name product** — avoid anything that could be counterfeit

### Research Tools & Methods
- **TikTok/Instagram:** Search product category + "where to buy" to find trending items
- **Amazon Best Sellers:** Look at subcategory bestsellers with fewer than 1,000 reviews (less competitive)
- **Etsy Trending:** Shows what's gaining traction in handmade/unique categories
- **Google Trends:** Validate whether interest is growing, stable, or declining
- **RealSourcing Factory Library:** Browse factory product catalogs to discover what's being manufactured

## Customer Service & Returns

### Setting Expectations
- Always display accurate shipping times (don't promise 5-day delivery if it takes 15–30 days)
- Include a clear return policy on your store
- Proactively send tracking information as soon as available

### Handling Disputes
- For damaged products: Request photos from customer, file claim with shipping carrier, offer replacement or refund
- For "not as described": Compare customer photos with product listing — if legitimate, offer refund
- For lost packages: Wait 5 business days past estimated delivery before declaring lost, then offer replacement

### Reducing Returns
- Accurate product descriptions and dimensions reduce "not as described" returns
- High-quality photos from multiple angles reduce "looks different in person" returns
- Clear assembly instructions reduce "too complicated" returns (especially for furniture)
